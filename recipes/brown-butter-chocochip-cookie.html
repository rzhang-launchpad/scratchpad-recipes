Brown Butter Chocolate Chip Cookie

Baking duration: 13 mins

Estimated Prep duration: 1 hour

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of flour
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter
  • 1 room temperature egg
  • 1 cup semi-sweet chocolate chips
  • 1/2 cup granulated white sugar
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp salt

Tools and when to use them...

  • whisk
    • to whisk the butter and sugar together
  • Wooden spoon
    • To mix the flour and chocolate chips in
    • To scrape the browned butter from the pot
  • Measuring spoon
    • to measure dry ingredients

Optional:

  • crushed nuts
  • additional egg yolk

Steps

Making the dough

  1. Brown butter on the stove, ~5 mins. Once it has been browned, let it cool down
  2. Transfer the browned butter to a mixing bowl and cream it together with the sugar. The ratio will be correct if the butter/sugar mixture keeps on getting stick in the whisk at the beginning
  3. Mix in vanilla and egg until just combined
  4. In a separate bowl, mix together the baking powder, salt, and flour together
  5. Slowly add in the dry ingredients a little at a time, using the wooden spoon to mix until combined. Add additional water as needed until texture of dough is correct
  6. Fold in chocolate chips
  7. Leave cookie dough in the fridge for at least 30 minutes, but ideally, overnight

Baking the cookies

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees
  2. Roll cookie dough into ~3cm wide balls and press them so they're a little flatter
  3. Place cookie dough balls onto parchment paper on a metal pan, 3 cm apart. These cookies do not spread very much while baking
  4. Bake cookies for 13 minutes or until the edges are slightly brown and the top is set
  5. Allow cookies to cool, then enjoyr
